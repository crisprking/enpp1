### Freedom-to-operate reading

- **Sulfamide leads (CHEMBL5826130, CHEMBL5915707)** map to a **granted US
  composition-of-matter patent, US-11591313-B2**, plus continuation applications
  (US-2021/0253556-A1, US-2023/0183212-A1). These are the *validated chemotype* —
  strong precedent but an occupied IP space; any program on this scaffold works
  around existing claims.
- **Hydroxamate leads (CHEMBL6149286, CHEMBL6174154)** appear only in a **single
  2024 application (US-2024/0383893-A1)** — earlier-stage IP, more white space,
  but the hydroxamate warhead carries the metal-binding liability documented in
  the selectivity analysis (direct Zn coordination drives anti-selectivity).
- **CHEMBL5555976 (sulfonamide)** has **no PubChem patent cross-reference** —
  either genuinely unclaimed chemical space or simply not yet indexed.
- **TZV (native inhibitor)** sits in a broad international family
  (WO-2019046778-A1 → US/EP/AU/BR/CA/KR national phase), the reference
  competitor's IP footprint.

**Strategic implication:** the piperidinol analog (AN10) advanced in the analog
step is built on the sulfamide scaffold. It inherits both the granted-patent
precedent (de-risking chemistry) and the need for a defensible composition
distinct from US-11591313-B2 claims.
